TrustSec AI Lab

By Nour Alhussien

Mission

Develop robust and resilient artificial intelligence (AI) models to detect, withstand, and mitigate emerging AI-driven cyber threats. The TrustSec AI Lab advances research at the intersection of AI and cybersecurity, building AI-driven solutions that enhance the security, robustness, and trustworthiness of interconnected digital systems.

Foundational Work

We advance core research in machine learning (ML) and deep learning (DL) to identify vulnerabilities and build defenses against adversarial AI systems, with focus areas including:

  • Network security: Threat detection across complex, distributed environments.
  • Data leakage: Preventing sensitive information exposure and model inversion.
  • Adversarial AI: Attacks, robustness analysis, and certified defenses.
  • Large Language Models (LLMs): Research focus on risks, attacks, and secure deployment.

Research Focus

  • Adversarial robustness: Design and evaluate defense mechanisms to protect AI systems (e.g., fraud detection, risk scoring, intrusion detection) from adversarial attacks.
  • IoT privacy in health: Enhance privacy in health-centric IoT ecosystems using generative models (e.g., diffusion) for anonymization and differential privacy for rigorous protections.
  • LLM hallucination & privacy: Verify retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) pipelines and apply secure multi-party computation (MPC) to reduce leakage and mitigate hallucinations.

Why It Matters

AI powers decision-making across sectors by processing vast, high-velocity data streams. The TrustSec AI Lab ensures these systems remain reliable, robust, and worthy of trust, safeguarding societal impact and mission-critical operations.

Collaboration (CEHC & Partners)

UAlbany research labs

We welcome collaborations to:

  • Employ DL and transfer learning for data curation and multi-source model training.
  • Strengthen privacy in health IoT via federated learning and differential privacy.
  • Apply behavioral analytics to detect cyber and physical threats and enable proactive defenses.
  • Build anomaly-based detection systems to support red teams across interconnected systems.

Government agencies: Apply ML to assess AI systems' susceptibility to phishing attacks, especially during environmental and societal crises.

Students: The lab is open to undergraduate and graduate students with Python experience who want to explore top-trending research topics in trustworthy AI and security.

Potential Impact on Education

  • Teach defense strategies against adversarial ML attacks on AI-powered systems.
  • Assess vulnerabilities in AI models used in CEHC-affiliated research.
  • Equip students with DL skills to design, evaluate, and deploy robust AI.
  • Conduct behavioral analytics with industry partners to detect and mitigate fraud.
  • Foster collaborations with private industry specializing in cybersecurity and AI.
